Timeline of the Effect of the Pandemic on Sports.

Friday, March 6th

The John's Hopkins versus Yeshiva basketball game was played without a single fan in the stadium due to one of the Yeshiva player's testing positive for Covid-19. Coronavirus was not a major threat to the sporting world yet.

Saturday, March 7th

The Golden State Warriors hosted the Philadelphia 76ers in San Francisco. They still had fans at their games as again, Coronavirus was not quite spreading faster then light yet. Golden State did however, put signs up everywhere warning fans that attending the game might increase their risk of contracting Covid-19.

Sunday, March 8th

The Indian Wells tennis tournament is often called the fifth major of tennis because it is such a big tournament. Players, including Venus Williams and Rafael Nadal, were already on site practicing when Indian Wells became the first major sporting event to cancel to try to hault the spread of Covid-19.

Monday, March 9th

Most sporting seasons still were not cancelled yet, but the MLB, NBA, NHL, and MLS all closed their locker room to outside personell. This meant no press and no one who didn't work directly or closely with the team. The sporting world sitll seemed pretty normal at this point and most assumed that the Coronavirus would just blow over.

Tuesday, March 10th

This was the day that the Ivy League became the first NCAA conference to not play any of their winter sports for the remainder of the winter season. From the standpoint of a student-athlete, no one understood why. Not many people knew how serious the Coronavirus actually was, and this seemed like an overreaction. We would soon see in days, and months to come that this was not an overreaction at all. Many tournaments across other leagues and conferences were announcing play without fans.

Wednesday, March 11th

The state of the pandemic became real to most players and athletes. Most leagues announced that their games would be played with no fans. The Ivy league, ahead of everyone again, went agead and cancelled their spring season as well. This now made other conferences, including our ACC, worry that they would soon follow. The NHL played five games that night, most major collegiate conferences announced they would continue play without fans, but the NBA had suspended their season.

Thursday, March 12th

This is the day everything took a turn downhill. The ACC and most other major conferences suspended their seasons. The ATP tennis tour suspended tournament play. The MLS suspended its season. The PGA tour cancelled its season. Players and athletes were on planes or at competition events and the plug was being pulled on their competition. Professional athletes were starting to test positive for Coronvirus and sports would not be the same for a very long time.

Friday, March 13th

In typical Friday the 13th fashion, this day was not a good one for the sporting world. The Masters was postponed, NASCAR was suspended, the Boston Marathon was postponed. Late the night before all other major leagues had cancelled their season. It was now clear to everyone that the pandemic was real and that the world was about to change.

Today

Most leagues and sports are back competing with either zero or limited fan attendance. The sporting world is adjusting to the new normal and hopefully will be back to normal soon.
